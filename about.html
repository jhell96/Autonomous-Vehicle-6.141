About 6.141/16.405

Robotics: Science and Systems (RSS) is a class that teaches introductory concepts of robotics and topics involved in the field. The class is taught through technical lectures, communication lectures, and through laboratory exercises to increase familiarity with the mechanical systems that will be used to complete the challenge project. The class is offered in Course 6: Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, and in Course 16: Aerospace Engineering.

Technical lectures are given each week to cover theory, including the topics of embedded systems and control, computer vision, localization, mapping, and planning algorithms. Practice of the presented theory is done during laboratory assignments, with the end goal of applying the learned concepts to an open-ended design and implementation challenge of racing a RC car around the MIT tunnel system. This advanced laboratory project is based significantly on the DARPA Urban Challenge, in which an MIT team competed and completed the 60-mile legal road course with a fully autonomous vehicle.

There is also a communication component to the class to improve technical communication ability through verbal and written reports. This communication component is heavily focused on helping students effectively contribute to a team project both technically and organizationally, though there are also opportunities for individual learning and communication.
